Types of kits

Clinical diagnostic kits
packages containing tools and reagents necessary for conducting diagnostic tests in clinical settings. They typically include items such as test strips, reagents, controls, and instruments needed to detect specific biomarkers or pathogens indicative of various diseases or conditions. Clinical diagnostic kits are crucial for medical professionals in accurately diagnosing illnesses and monitoring patient health.
Life science kits
Life science kits encompass a broad range of kits designed for research and experimentation in the field of biology, encompassing disciplines such as molecular biology, genetics, cell biology, and biochemistry. These kits often contain reagents, enzymes, buffers, and other materials necessary for performing experiments, such as DNA extraction, PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction), protein analysis, cell culture, or immunohistochemistry. Life science kits are widely used in academic research, pharmaceutical development, biotechnology, and industrial applications to study biological systems and advance scientific knowledge.
